The Final Report of the Royal Commission into the Robodebt Scheme, released on July 7, 2023, is a damning indictment of a "costly failure of public administration" that was "neither fair nor legal". It describes the initiative as a "gross betrayal" and a "human tragedy" that caused significant distress and traumatized vulnerable Australians. 
Below is a breakdown of the key text, findings, and themes from the report:
Key Findings and Language
"Crude and Cruel": The report explicitly describes the scheme as a "crude and cruel mechanism" that made people feel like criminals.
Unlawful and Unfair: The scheme was found to not comply with the income calculation provisions of the Social Security Act and lacked procedural fairness.
Political Motivation: The report highlights a culture where the scheme was pushed to meet political narratives of "crackdowns on rorting," despite warnings about its illegality.
Misleading Advice: Former Prime Minister Scott Morrison was found to have misled Cabinet by withholding relevant information regarding the scheme's legality.
"Systemic Injustice": The report outlines how an over-reliance on data and automation, combined with a lack of human oversight, resulted in false debts. 
Core Recommendations
The report made 57 recommendations to ensure such a scheme is never repeated, including: 
Strengthening Accountability: Increasing accountability for Senior Executive Service (SES) public servants.
Improving Process: Ensuring policies are designed with an emphasis on the people they are meant to serve.
Legal Scrutiny: Requiring that New Policy Proposals include standard, specific statements about legal risks and that underlying legal advice is considered.
Data Oversight: Increasing review and oversight of automated decision-making. 
Impact and Scope
Financial Damage: The scheme unlawfully raised $1.76 billion in alleged debts against approximately 526,000 Australians.
Human Cost: The report emphasizes the devastating, ongoing effects on individuals, including distress, trauma, and hardship.
Compensation: Following the report, the government initiated further compensation, with total payouts reaching over $2.4 billion. 
Specific Quotes and Document Details
Volume: The report comprises three volumes.
Sealed Section: A special, sealed section of the report recommended referrals for civil and criminal prosecution for specific individuals.
Public Service Failures: The report noted that public servants acted with a lack of openness, courage, and curiosity, creating a culture where warnings were ignored. 
The Australian Government accepted all 56 of the public recommendations.
